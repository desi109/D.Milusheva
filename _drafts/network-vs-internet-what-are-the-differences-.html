What is a Network?

The network is two, three or more connected computers, servers, or other devices which share data resource like an internet connection, printers, etc.

Which are the Main Types of Networks?

There are four main types of networks, depending on their sizeÂ :

  • LAN - Local Area Network

    LAN is a group of computers, which are connected together with each other in a small network such as building, office.

  • PAN - Personal Area Network

    PAN is a network within a range of about 10 meters, for example, in your home, which is used for connecting personal devices like computers, laptops, mobile phones, media players, or even play stations.

  • MAN - Metropolitan Area Network

    A metropolitan area network is a network that covers a larger geographic area by interconnecting a different LAN to form a larger network.

  • Government agencies use MAN to connect to the citizens and private industries.
  • In MAN, various LANs are connected to each other through a telephone exchange line.
  • The most widely used protocols in MAN are RS-232, Frame Relay, ATM, ISDN, OC-3, ADSL, etc.
  • It has a higher range than Local Area Network(LAN).

  • WAN - Wide Area Network

    A Wide Area Network is a network that extends over a large geographical area such as states or countries.

  • A Wide Area Network is quite bigger network than the LAN.
  • A Wide Area Network is not limited to a single location, but it spans over a large geographical area through a telephone line, fibre optic cable or satellite links.
  • The internet is one of the biggest WAN in the world.
  • A Wide Area Network is widely used in the field of Business, government, and education.
